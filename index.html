About





I am a second-year Ph.D. student at the Computer Science Department of Columbia University in New York City.
My advisors are Luis Gravano and Daniel Hsu.
Before coming to Columbia, I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer at Behavioral Signals.
I received a M.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA).

My research interests lie in the fields of Information Extraction, Natural Language Processing, and Machine Learning. Currently, I am working on Weakly Supervised Learning methods for fine-grained classification of online text reviews. I am interested in applying these methods for problems such as opinion mining or applications related to public health. More generally, I am interested in effective methods for combining information from heterogenous sources or multiple sense modalities.

I love playing funk n' jazz on my bass guitar, windsurfing, taking pictures, and traveling to new places.
