About





I am a second-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Columbia University in the City of New York.
Before coming to Columbia, I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer at Behavioral Signals.
I received a M.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA).

My research interests lie in the fields of Information Extraction, Natural Language Processing, and Machine Learning.
Currently, I am employing weakly supervised learning frameworks in deep learning models for a fine-grained analysis of online user-generated reviews without using fine-grained ground-truth labels, which are expensive to obtain manually.
I am interested in applying these methods for problems such as opinion mining and for applications related to public health.
More generally, I am interested in the development of effective methods for combining information from multiple heterogenous sources and modalities, so that we can accurately model complex events and take concrete actions in the real-world.

In addition to doing research, I love playing funk n' jazz on my bass guitar, windsurfing, taking photos, and traveling.
For more information about me, please see my CV or contact me.
