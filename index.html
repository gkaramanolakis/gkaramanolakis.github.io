About





I am a second-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Columbia University in the City of New York. My advisors are Prof. Luis Gravano, and Prof. Daniel Hsu.

My research interests lie in the fields of Machine Learning, Information Extraction, and Natural Language Processing. Currently, I am employing weakly supervised learning frameworks in deep learning models for a fine-grained analysis of text documents. I am interested in applying these methods for problems such as opinion mining from online reviews, and for applications related to public health. More generally, I am interested in the development of effective methods for combining information from multiple heterogenous sources and modalities, so that we can accurately model complex events and take concrete actions in the real world.

In addition to doing research, I love playing funk n' jazz on my bass guitar, windsurfing, taking photos, and traveling.

For more information about me, please see my CV or contact me.

Papers



Pending Submission (double-blind)

Giannis Karamanolakis, Daniel Hsu, and Luis Gravano
WSDM 2019, Melbourne, Australia
In summary, we employ Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) in deep learning models (CNNs, RNNs) for sentence-level classification without using sentence-level ground-truth labels. Instead, we use review-level labels as a weak source of supervision. We propose to estimate the MIL aggregation function using a sigmoid attention mechanism. We apply our proposed model for the fine-grained sentiment analysis of reviews, and for the discovery of foodborne illness in online restaurant reviews.
